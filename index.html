Hi! I'm Chrissie.

I am a natural problem solver who uncovered my passion for programming two years ago when I discovered the online webcasts and course materials for Stanford University's three introductory CS courses. With the help of Stack Overflow I eagerly worked my way through Programming Methodology (taught in Java), Programming Paradigms in C++, and Programming Abstractions in C.

At the time, I was working for Cengage Learning developing online educational materials. I wrote scripts to automate repetitive tasks, built a GUI game using teaching students about arbitrage, and wrote a program to recursively assemble lists of vocabulary words into crossword puzzle formations. I learned SQL to help out the support team with customer queries. Along the way I took Coursera courses on algorithms and mathematical thinking, did tutorials in JavaScript and HTML, and got comfortable with Unix.

I began programming fulltime as an intern on the software engineering team at an Urban Planning firm, Calthorpe Associates. There I did data visualization with D3.js, and learned Python, Django, PostgreSQL.

I’ve spent the past 12 weeks at the programming boot camp AppAcademy learning the full stack of web development (Ruby, Rails, JavaScript, Backbone, Node), while also focusing on best practices like pair programming, testing, and object-oriented design.

When I’m not coding I can be found biking or on a walk, cooking a delicious meal with friends, or teaching myself to play banjo.
